Which National Football Team To Support?

In 2016 the NFL team of St. Louis relocated to Los Angeles and became the Los Angeles Rams. This left many fans based in the St. Louis area asking the question “what do you do when your pro sports team moves away?” This page is designed for those unlucky ones searching for a new team and new spectators of the sport. You can also use this page if you have become disloyal to your current team and looking for a new team to support.

There are 32 teams to pick from and here is data to help you choose which team to support

The 32 NFL (national Football League) teams split into two categories, the NFC (National Football Conference) and the AFC (American Football Conference). The NFL is one of the four major professional sports leagues in North America, and the highest professional level of American football in the world. The NFL's 17-week regular season runs from early September to late December, with each team playing 16 games and having one bye week (week off). Following the conclusion of the regular season, six teams from each conference (four division winners and two wild card teams) advance to the playoffs, a single-elimination tournament culminating in the Super Bowl, which is usually held in the first Sunday in February, and is played between the champions of the NFC and AFC.

The 32 teams have been graded in 11 categories taken from ESPN the magazine's long-running "Ultimate Standings" (which use surveys conducted by opinion research firms in a similar attempt to quantify the benefits of rooting for each pro franchise in the U.S. and Canada).
Grading system - results are out of 100.

Categories

Coaching - Strength of on-field leadership
Players - Effort on the field, likability off them
Uniform - Stylishness of uniform design, according to Uni Watch's Paul Lukas
Stadium experience - Quality of venue; fan-friendliness of environment; frequency of game-day promotions

Categories

Tradition - Championships/division titles/wins in team's entire history
Fan relations - Courtesy by players, coaches and front offices toward fans, and how well a team uses technology to reach them
Ownership - Honesty; loyalty to core players and the community

Bandwagon Factor

Bandwagon Factor - Are the team's next 5 years likely to be better than their previous 5?

Future Wins

Future wins - Projected wins over next 5 seasons

Bang For The Buck

Bang for the buck - Wins per fan dollars spent

Affordability

Affordability - Price of tickets, parking and concessions
